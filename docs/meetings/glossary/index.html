THE TWELVE STEPS ARE FOUND ON PAGES 59 AND 60 OF THE BOOK

ALCOHOLICS ANONYMOUS. FOLLOWING ARE THE PAGES WHERE EACH

STEP IS DESCRIBED AND THE DIRECTIONS FOR TAKING THE STEPS ARE

LOCATED.

Step 1 We admitted we were powerless over alcohol—that our lives had become

unmanageable. This step is described on pages xxiii xxx; I 43. The directions for taking Step I are on page 10. para. 2, lines 13.

Step 2 Came to believe that a Power greater than ourselves could restore us to sanity.

This step is described on pages 44 60. The directions for taking Step 2 are on page 47, para. 2. lines 13.

Step 3 Made a decision to turn our will and our lives over to the care of God as we

Understood Him. This step is descrcribed on pages 60 63. The directions for taking Step 3 are on page 63. para. 2. lines 18.

Step 4 Made a searching and fearless moral inventory of ourselves.

This step is described on pages 63 7 1. The directions for taking Step 4 are on page 64, para. 1. lines 19, para. 2, lines 16.

Step 5 Admitted to God, to ourselves, and to another human being the exact nature of

our wrongs. This step is described on pages 72 75 The directions for taking Step 5 are on page 75, para. 1, lines 14; para. 2, lines 12.

Step 6 Were entirely ready to have God remove all these defects of character.

This step is described on pages 7576. The directions for taking Step 6 are on page 76, para. 1, lines 35.

Step 7 Humbly asked Him to remove our shortcomings.

Step 8 Made a list of all persons we had harmed, and became willing to make amends

to them all. This step is described on page 76. The directions for taking Step 8 are on page 76, para, 3, lines 35.

God as we Understood Him, praying only for knowledge of His will for us and the power to carry that out. This step is described on pages 85 88. The directions for taking Step 11 are on page 86, para. 2, lines 15; para. 3, lines 16.

this message to alcoholics, and to practice these Principles in all our affairs. This step is described on Pages 89 103 and pages 569570 The directions for taking Step 12 are on pages 89, para. 1, lines 17. (Specific suggestions for conducting twelfth step calls are found throughout pages 89103).

In an attempt to discover the meaning of the words used in our basis text book Alcoholics Anonymous

when it was written, we have used many sources for this glossary. Our primary source was Webster’s Daily

Use Dictionary published in 1934. For the historical references we used the World Book Encyclopedia and

Encarta Encyclopedia. Because our language has changed much since our “Big Book” was written, and

because the book’s purpose is to explain, “precisely how we have recovered”, we believe it is important to

25

understand what the words meant when they were written, not how the have been filtered by our society

today. This glossary is not complete by any means but is meant to be helpful.
